# SmartRent Considerations — Channel Partner Portal & Outside Activity

> **Not legal advice.** This doc summarizes the SmartRent Employee Handbook (Oct 2025) provisions that apply to **five** of our six founders/recruits (Jonathan VP Solutions, Justin VP BD, Braeden VP Product, Wil Director Smart Communities, **Halim Director Engineering**) and proposes disposition options for **Channel Partner Portal** specifically. Tim Elias is the only founder fully independent of SmartRent. Tim's counsel — or any qualified attorney — must review before the team commits to a disposition.

---

## TL;DR

- **Five of six** founders are current SmartRent leadership: Jonathan (VP Solutions), Justin (VP BD), Braeden (VP Product), Wil (Director Smart Communities), and **Halim (Director Engineering)**. Tim is the only fully-independent founder.
- The SmartRent handbook contains **no traditional non-compete clause** (consistent with FTC's 2024 guidance weakening non-competes), but does contain **three overlapping conflict-of-interest provisions** that apply to outside business activity by current employees.
- **Most apps in the portfolio** (Real Tech GTM, Rovian, Wedding Planner, Kith, edgenote, Baby Tracker, DealPayment, Cannabis HighTide, Rehabit, Life Events Suite) are in markets unrelated to SmartRent — **low risk under §1.10**.
- **Channel Partner Portal** is the exception. A multifamily operator-vendor portal lives close to SmartRent's stated business: *"developing software and hardware that empowers property owners, managers, and homebuilders to effectively manage, protect and automate daily operations processes."* This is **a §1.6 conflict-of-interest concern** at minimum, and arguably a **§3.5 corporate-opportunity issue** if SmartRent customer relationships or insights are used in the build / GTM.
- **Real Tech Consulting (parent)** is generally permissible under §1.10's outside-employment rule **provided** it doesn't compete with SmartRent and doesn't solicit SmartRent customers.
- **Halim's Director-Engineering role at SmartRent** doesn't change the per-app risk reads (engineering work on apps in unrelated markets is permitted under §1.10), but it does narrow the "concept research" carve-out: only Tim can do early-stage Channel Portal research without triggering §1.6.

## The three handbook clauses that apply

Source: *SmartRent Employee Handbook, Revised October 2025* (the "**Handbook**"). Page references below.

### §1.6 — Conflicts of Interest (p. 7–8)

> *"A conflict of interest occurs when an individual's private interest (or the interest of a member of his or her family) interferes, or even appears to interfere, with the interests of the Company as a whole."*

Examples of conflicts the Handbook calls out specifically:

- **Being employed by, or acting as a consultant to, a competitor or potential competitor, supplier, contractor, client, or customer**, regardless of the nature of the employment, while also being employed by SmartRent.
- **Owning or having a significant financial interest in a competitor**, supplier, or contractor.

Disposition: *"Conflicts of interest should be avoided unless specifically authorized as described in this Section 1.6."* Authorization requires written description to the Compliance Officer + the Compliance Officer's written approval.

### §1.10 — Outside Employment (p. 16)

> *"Employees may hold outside jobs with companies that do not compete with SmartRent as long as they meet the performance standards of their jobs with SmartRent."*

> *"Outside employment that competes with [or] constitutes a conflict of interest with or creates an adverse impact on SmartRent is **prohibited**."*

Plus: cannot use Company time, equipment, or supplies for outside work; cannot sell related products/services to the Company; cannot interfere with primary duties.

### §3.5 — Corporate Opportunities (p. 17)

> *"Employees owe a duty of loyalty to the Company and must advance the Company's legitimate interests when the opportunity to do arises. Employees are prohibited from taking for themselves personally (or for the benefit of friends or family members) opportunities that are discovered through the use of Company assets, property, information, or position."*

> *"In addition, no Employee may compete with the Company. As a result, **unless approved by the Legal Department**, you should not:*
> - *own a direct or indirect interest in any competitor of the Company;*
> - *take opportunities that are discovered through the use of any Company property, information, or position;*
> - *give to others any business opportunity in which the employee knows the Company would be interested."*

This is the most explicit clause and the one that bites Channel Partner Portal hardest. **Authorization by SmartRent Legal is the named carve-out.**

## Disposition options for Channel Partner Portal

Four paths, presented in order from most conservative to most aggressive. **The team picks one — and the picked path becomes part of the Path-A vote on the recommendation slide.**

### Path A — Drop it

- Removes the §1.6 / §3.5 conflict entirely.
- Loses what is arguably the strongest founder-market-fit bet in the portfolio.
- Replaces Path A on the recommendation slide with: **Real Tech GTM + (Rovian or Life Events Suite)**.
- **Conservative.** Recommended if any of the 5 SmartRent founders is unwilling to take §3.5 risk, or if Tim's counsel reads the §3.5 clause strictly.

### Path B — Defer to post-departure

- The **5 SmartRent founders** (Jonathan, Justin, Braeden, Wil, Halim) do **zero work** on Channel Partner Portal until they have departed SmartRent.
- Tim alone is fully independent — but Tim's lane is crypto/legal/LATAM, not multifamily-portal engineering. Realistically he can't carry the build alone.
- The portal lives as a "post-spin-out" project: starts only after the departures are public.
- **Trade-off:** loses 6–12 months of build time. Probably moves Channel Portal off the **first** top-2 vote; revisit at the 6-month checkpoint.

### Path C — Get SmartRent Legal Department approval (recommended)

- §3.5 explicitly carves out *"unless approved by the Legal Department."* §1.6 has the same Compliance-Officer-written-approval mechanism.
- Each of the **5 SmartRent founders** (Jonathan, Justin, Braeden, Wil, Halim) submits a written description of the activity to SmartRent's Compliance Officer / Legal Department and seeks written authorization.
- Authorization will likely come with conditions: no SmartRent customer solicitation, no use of SmartRent confidential information, no SmartRent time / equipment / supplies, the activity does not "compete" or "create adverse impact."
- **Best of both worlds** if SmartRent grants approval. **Worst case** if denied: the team falls back to Path A or B for Channel Portal, but the rest of the portfolio is unaffected.
- This is **the recommended starting move** — get the answer in writing before committing the team direction.

### Path D — Re-scope away from overlap

- Pivot Channel Partner Portal away from multifamily property management into an adjacent-but-non-overlapping vertical:
  - **Commercial real estate** (office, retail, industrial)
  - **HOA / condo associations** (residential but a different buyer)
  - **Hospitality chains** (hotel facilities management)
  - **Single-family rental** at scale (Roofstock-style, but vendor-side)
- Lower founder-market-fit (some of the SmartRent founders' relationships don't carry over) but lower §1.6 / §3.5 exposure.
- Could be combined with Path C: get Legal Dept approval AND scope to a non-overlapping vertical to belt-and-suspenders.

## Operating rules until disposition is chosen

These apply now, regardless of which path the team picks:

1. **No SmartRent customer relationships are activated** for any Channel Partner Portal pilot conversation. Justin's "5 named pilot conversations" milestone in the 90-day plan is paused until disposition is settled.
2. **Tim is the only fully-independent founder.** He may do concept research, market sizing, and competitor analysis on the portal idea without triggering §1.6 (he isn't a SmartRent employee).
3. **Halim is now bound** by the same handbook clauses as Jonathan, Justin, Braeden, and Wil. He may continue building on apps in unrelated markets (Real Tech GTM, Rovian, Wedding, Kith, edgenote, DealPayment, Cannabis HighTide, Baby Tracker, Rehabit, Life Events Suite — all permitted under §1.10 because none compete with SmartRent), but **must not write Channel Portal code or do Channel Portal product work** until a disposition is chosen.
4. The **5 SmartRent founders** may **discuss the concept** in cofounder meetings under the Mutual NDA, but **may not contribute SmartRent confidential information, customer-list data, or product-roadmap intel**. The Mutual NDA's third-party IP protection clause (§7) explicitly prevents this and the SmartRent handbook (§2.7 — Safeguarding Company Information) requires it.
5. **The deck (this Reveal.js build) does NOT name specific SmartRent customers** as Channel Portal pilot targets. Slides 14 and 23 reference "warm intros via the SmartRent network" generically; no specific operator name.
6. **No use of SmartRent equipment, time, or systems** for any Real Tech work — the deck is built on personal hardware, on personal time, with personal accounts.

## Real Tech Consulting (parent brand) — separate read

Real Tech Consulting (`realtechconsultant.com`) sells modern web/app builds, AI integrations, GTM automation, fractional CTO/CPO, and technical due diligence. None of those services compete with SmartRent's product.

- **§1.10:** generally permissible if disclosed to manager + HR and doesn't interfere with primary duties.
- **§3.5:** acceptable provided no SmartRent customers / opportunities are solicited.

**Recommended actions:**
1. Jonathan (and any other SmartRent-employed founder doing client-facing consulting work) provides written disclosure to their SmartRent manager + HR. Template language: *"I provide consulting services through Real Tech Consulting LLC. The services do not compete with SmartRent's products or services and are performed entirely on personal time using personal equipment. I will not solicit any SmartRent customer or use any SmartRent confidential information."*
2. Keep a clear separation between SmartRent customers and Real Tech Consulting prospects. If a target is **also** a SmartRent customer, route through Path C (written authorization).

## How this affects the Mutual NDA

The Mutual NDA at [`NDA-Real-Tech-Mutual.md`](NDA-Real-Tech-Mutual.md) already addresses two of the three handbook concerns:

- **§7 Third-Party Confidential Information** explicitly prevents any party from disclosing SmartRent confidential information into the cofounder discussions. This protects the **5 SmartRent founders** from inadvertently breaching §2.7 of the SmartRent handbook.
- **§6 Mutual Non-Solicitation** has carve-outs for general advertising and pre-existing relationships, so it doesn't conflict with SmartRent's customer relationships either way.

**What the NDA does NOT cover** — and what this document is for:

- The §1.10 / §3.5 question of whether **Channel Partner Portal itself** can be pursued at all by current SmartRent employees.
- The disposition decision (Paths A / B / C / D above) is a **team decision**, separate from the NDA, and should be documented in writing and stored alongside the executed NDAs.
