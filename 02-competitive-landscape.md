## 11. Life Events Suite (Bundle)

The bundle's competitor isn't a single app — it's the **status quo of using 3 separate tools** at different life stages, each with its own signup, billing, and onboarding.

| Substitute | Why couples use it now | Why bundle wins |
|------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| The Knot + WhatsApp + Huckleberry | Default of least effort | Three signups; three billing relationships; data doesn't carry over |
| Zola + iCloud Family + Glow | Better-design-conscious users | Same — fragmented |
| DIY: Notion + Google Photos + Notes | Tech-forward couples | Lots of work; no automation |

**Our opening:** "One signup. One bill. Wedding becomes Kith. Kith stays. Baby Tracker activates. Real Tech is your platform for the next decade — not a 6-month tool."

## Cross-cutting Observations

1. **Real Tech's "everywhere" pattern:** Across nearly every category, our wedge is the same — **vertical depth + integrated AI + simpler pricing**. That's a brand insight, not just a product strategy.

2. **The strongest IP moats:**
   - Patent-pending DealPayment Token + ELT escrow process (DealPayment)
   - SmartRent leadership network for warm-intro distribution (Channel Partner)
   - On-device AI architecture (edgenote)
   - Founder origin story (Kith)

3. **Categories where we're commodity-vulnerable:**
   - Baby Tracker solo (do not pursue solo — bundle only)
   - Rehabit SaaS (PropStream's data depth is hard to match)

4. **Categories where we have white space:**
   - Channel Partner Portal (no direct multifamily-focused competitor exists)
   - DealPayment (patent + state-compliance combo is genuinely novel)
   - edgenote (privacy-first prosumer wedge is uncrowded)
