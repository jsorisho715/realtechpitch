# 07 — Brand Architecture

How "Real Tech" relates to the 10 product brands. When to lead with parent vs. product. The naming rules.

---

## The Two-Layer Model

**Layer 1 — Parent brand:** Real Tech LLC (realtechconsultant.com). Consulting firm + product studio. Sells expertise, builds custom, ships its own products.

**Layer 2 — Product brands:** Standalone identities that don't always need "Real Tech" in the name.

## When to Lead with Parent Brand

**Lead with "Real Tech LLC" when:**

1. Selling consulting services (advisory, custom builds, fractional CTO)
2. The customer is buying *a team*, not a product
3. The pitch is "we shipped 10 things, look at our portfolio"
4. Speaking to investors, partners, or recruits about the studio model
5. The product is a tool we built for ourselves and decided to sell (Real Tech GTM is the prototype)

**Tone:** Premium, technical, confident, multifamily-fluent. Inter + Fraunces. Navy + Gold gradient.

---

## When to Lead with Product Brand

**Lead with the product brand when:**

1. The buyer is in a vertical that doesn't care about Real Tech (a plumber buying Rovian doesn't need to know about consulting)
2. The product has its own moat that needs to be the headline (DealPayment's patent, Kith's emotional positioning, edgenote's privacy stance)
3. SEO / category presence requires a domain-as-brand match (rovian.ai, edgenote.ai, dealpayment.com)
4. The product can outgrow Real Tech (acquisition target potential)

**Tone per product:** Different from the parent. Each product brand has its own voice (see "Voice Per Brand" below).

## Naming Rules

### Real Tech-prefixed names

Use when the product is **closely tied to consulting + custom-build value prop**:

- **Real Tech GTM** — the productized version of how we run Real Tech itself
- **Real Tech Wedding** (or "Real Tech Wedding Studio") — the Wedding Planner AI when sold to wedding professionals
- **Real Tech Family** (proposed brand for the Life Events Suite bundle)

These names broadcast "Real Tech does this for itself; you can buy what we use."

### Independent brand names

Use when the product needs **its own market identity**:

- **Rovian.ai** — service-biz buyers don't need to know it's a Real Tech product
- **DealPayment.com** — finance/crypto buyers want a focused brand, not a consulting firm
- **Kith / startakith.com** — emotional brand; the Real Tech name would dilute the product's story
- **edgenote.ai** — privacy product; some buyers will be allergic to "tech consulting" framing
- **HighTide** — cannabis category needs its own positioning (Real Tech parent brand could create banking issues for partners)
- **Rehabit** — real estate name lives on its own
- **Channel Partner Portal** — needs a real brand name (working title; needs naming work). Candidates: *"Tier"* / *"OpenChannel"* / *"Multifamily Bench"* / *"Bidwell"* — all candidates with the team

### When the parent brand is mentioned within product brands

Use a small "by Real Tech LLC" attribution in:
- Product website footers
- App about-screens
- Legal / privacy / terms pages
- Press / news articles

Never lead with parent brand on a product surface unless the product brand is itself a Real Tech-prefixed name.

## When We Acquire / Get Acquired

A clean brand architecture matters most when **someone wants to buy a single product**.

| Scenario | What it looks like |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Vapi acquires Rovian.ai** | Clean — Rovian has its own brand and we keep Real Tech parent + remaining apps |
| **A multifamily PMS company acquires Channel Partner Portal** | Clean — Portal is its own brand; SmartRent veterans stay on as the team or transition |
| **A wedding-tech rollup acquires Real Tech Wedding** | We lose the "Real Tech" prefix; product gets a new name post-acquisition |
| **A privacy-first SaaS acquires edgenote.ai** | Clean — edgenote is its own brand |
| **All of Real Tech is acquired** | Real Tech LLC is the parent acquired; product brands stay or fold under acquirer |

The architecture above is designed so any single product can be sold or merged without disturbing the others. That's a real strategic asset.

## Real Tech Consulting Positioning

The consulting business needs to be positioned as **"the firm that ships its own products"** — not "the firm that sells you our software."

### One-paragraph positioning statement (for sales reps to memorize):

> *"Real Tech is a multifamily-and-beyond product studio. We ship our own software — ten apps live, including a smart-home tool used by 850K+ multifamily homes via our team's prior work. We hire ourselves out to companies who want to move at our speed: AI integrations, full-stack builds, fractional product leadership, and consulting from people who actually ship."*

### Three-line elevator pitch:

> *"We're a studio. We've shipped ten products. The same team that builds them will build for you, faster than your incumbent vendor."*

### One-sentence:

> *"Real Tech is the studio behind ten apps — and we hire out."*

## What This Brand Architecture Unlocks

1. **Parallel sales motions** — Justin can sell Rovian to plumbers without confusing Real Tech consulting customers
2. **Consulting credibility** — every product is a case study; we don't have to invent portfolio examples
3. **Acquirer optionality** — products can be carved off without bringing down the parent
4. **Founder narrative** — "we ship for ourselves first, then sell" is a unique brand position vs. consultancies that just sell hours
5. **Talent magnet** — engineers and PMs want to work at studios with shipped products, not just service shops
